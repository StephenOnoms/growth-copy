01 Paid social · LinkedIn TOFU–MOFU

Three angles, one product: Revolut Business

A single-image LinkedIn campaign targeting founders and finance leads at SMEs trading internationally. Three variants testing three distinct motivations, written to isolate which pain converts.

Variant A · The cost angle

Your bank takes a cut every time you pay a supplier abroad.

Revolut Business gives you exchange at the interbank rate, accounts in 25+ currencies and one dashboard to see it all. The money you save is not a rounding error.

See your FX savings

Why: leads with a quantifiable pain. Built for cold audiences, where a number beats a feeling.

Variant B · The time angle

Finance admin is a full-time job. Yours, apparently.

Automate expenses, approvals and payments with Revolut Business. Close the month in hours, not weeks, and get back to running the company.

Start in minutes

Why: an emotional angle aimed at founders wearing the CFO hat. Tests humour against Variant A's hard numbers.

Variant C · The scale angle

Selling in 12 markets shouldn't mean 12 bank accounts.

Local accounts, global payments and team cards with controls built in. Revolut Business grows with you, wherever your customers are.

Open your account

Why: bottom-leaning copy for warm retargeting audiences already comparing multi-market solutions.

06 Ebook · Lead magnet MOFU

Long-form that feeds the funnel

A gated ebook concept for Ledgerly, designed to capture mid-funnel leads: founders running finance without a finance hire. Cover blurb, chapter structure and an excerpt from chapter one.

SPEC WORK — continues the fictional Ledgerly brand

Ledgerly Guides · No. 01

The CFO-less Company

A founder's guide to running finance before your first finance hire

Free download · 38 pages

Back-cover blurb

Most companies don't hire a finance lead until they pass 50 people. Until then, finance is whoever lost the coin toss. This guide is for that person: a plain-language playbook covering the systems, habits and guardrails that keep a growing company's money under control — without an accounting degree, and without giving up your evenings to a spreadsheet.

Chapters

  1. The accidental CFO: what you actually need to control (and what can wait)
  2. Cash visibility: the one dashboard worth building this week
  3. Spend policy without the policy document
  4. The month-end close, demystified in five steps
  5. When to outsource, when to automate, when to hire
  6. The handover: setting up your first finance hire to succeed

Excerpt — Chapter 1

Nobody plans to become the finance person. It happens the way most jobs happen at a startup: a thing needed doing, you were nearest to it, and now it's yours. Six months later you're approving expenses on a Sunday and wondering when, exactly, you agreed to this. Here's the good news: running finance at a growing company is not about knowing accounting. It's about controlling three numbers and building systems that watch them for you. This guide is about those three numbers.
